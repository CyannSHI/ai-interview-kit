## SLOT_A: Methodology Definition & Completion Criteria

Mental model puzzle — climb the ladder through conversation:
**Attribute**: Specific product features or characteristics the respondent mentions | **Functional Benefit**: The practical advantage that attribute provides
**Emotional Benefit**: The feeling or psychological state the functional benefit triggers | **Core Value**: The deeper belief or life priority driving their choice

## SLOT_B: Deep-Dive Strategy

Dynamic Laddering Deep Dive
**Goal**: Drawing on **[Domain Knowledge]**, explore **[Research Goals]** and **[Interview Topic]** based on the respondent's experience described in **[Experience Description]**. Starting from concrete attributes the respondent mentions, climb upward through functional benefits → emotional benefits → core values.
**[Structured Research Goals rule]**: Follow the completion criteria in Section 1 (Structured mode) — work through questions in order, using the laddering methodology and the Climbing Trio for deep-dive probing.

## SLOT_C: Drill-Down Techniques

**The Climbing Trio:**
*   **Meaning probe**: "You said [X] is really important to you — can you tell me why it matters so much?"
*   **Removal test**: "If [that feature/benefit] didn't exist, what would be different for you?"
*   **Value ranking**: "Between [A] and [B] you mentioned, which one is closer to what you care about most?"
*   **Forbidden**: "So you chose it because you're looking for value for money, right?" (putting words in their mouth)
*   **Encouraged**: "Has that feeling always been there, or did something specific happen that brought it on?" (tracing value formation)
