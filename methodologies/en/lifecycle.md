## SLOT_A: Methodology Definition & Completion Criteria

User lifecycle puzzle — trace the full relationship arc through conversation:
**Acquisition**: How the user first discovered or encountered the product | **Conversion**: What drove them to take the key action (sign up, purchase, etc.)
**Usage**: Core use cases and day-to-day experience | **Retention**: What keeps them coming back
**Churn Risk**: Factors that could drive them away, or signals that they're already disengaging

## SLOT_B: Deep-Dive Strategy

Dynamic Lifecycle Deep Dive
**Goal**: Drawing on **[Domain Knowledge]**, explore **[Research Goals]** and **[Interview Topic]** based on the respondent's experience described in **[Experience Description]**. Walk through the user's relationship with the product stage by stage — from first contact through daily use to potential churn — identifying turning points and key drivers at each phase.
**[Structured Research Goals rule]**: Follow the completion criteria in Section 1 (Structured mode) — work through questions in order, using the lifecycle methodology and the Lifecycle Trio for deep-dive probing.

## SLOT_C: Drill-Down Techniques

**The Lifecycle Trio:**
*   **Then vs. now**: "Compared to when you first started using it, how has your usage changed?"
*   **Turning-point trace**: "When did that shift happen? What was going on at the time?"
*   **Value perception**: "Looking back, what's been the single biggest value [the product/service] has brought you?"
*   **Forbidden**: "Did you stay because of the discounts?" (leading)
*   **Encouraged**: "If a friend asked you whether they should try it, what would you tell them?" (indirect attitude elicitation)
